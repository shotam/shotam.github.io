Shota Momma 門馬将太

I am a post-doctoral researcher at University of California, San Diego. I study sentence comprehension, sentence production, and the relationship between the two. I am specifically interested in how we assemble sentence structures in speaking and understanding. An overarching question in my research is whether we need two different mechanisms for assembling sentence structures for speaking and understanding, or just one single mechanism is sufficient. I use whatever methods that allow me to study the structural processes in comprehension and production, including new and existing behavioral methods and Electroencephalography. I especially like devising new experimental methods that allow me to examine the question that is hard to examine using existing methods.
